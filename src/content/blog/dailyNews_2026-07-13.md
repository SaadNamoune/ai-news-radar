### [Unified Mechanism and Plug-and-Play Loss for Knowledge Distillation via Interaction Sparsification](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08776)

<small>**cs.LG updates on arXiv.org** &nbsp;·&nbsp; 2026-07-13 05:00:00 &nbsp;·&nbsp; `knowledge-distillation` `transformers` `model-compression` `interaction-analysis`</small>

**Overview:** This paper investigates the underlying mechanism of knowledge distillation (KD) in LLMs, proposing a unified framework based on interaction decomposition. It reveals that diverse KD methods share a common mechanism: sparsification of interactions, where student models retain only essential interactions while suppressing others. **Method:** The authors decompose LLM output scores into interactions (nonlinear relationships among input variables) and analyze how KD methods enforce sparsity. They introduce the Complex Interaction Penalty (CIP) loss, a plug-and-play function that explicitly penalizes complex interactions during distillation. **Results:** Experiments across in-domain and OOD benchmarks show that integrating CIP improves performance across diverse KD methods. The framework explains performance variance as a function of a method's ability to handle complex interactions. **Impact:** Advances the theoretical understanding of KD mechanisms and provides a practical tool (CIP) to enhance distillation efficiency, with implications for model compression and transfer learning.

### [Lattice Traversal Framework for Sound and Complete Adversarial Robustness Certifications in MLPs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08773)

<small>**cs.AI updates on arXiv.org** &nbsp;·&nbsp; 2026-07-13 05:00:00 &nbsp;·&nbsp; `adversarial-robustness` `lattice-traversal` `MLP-verification` `certification-theory`</small>

**Overview:** Introduces a rigorous theoretical framework reducing adversarial robustness to lattice traversal, addressing both sound and complete certifications for MLP classifiers. This work advances AI safety by formalizing robustness guarantees beyond traditional sound-only approaches. **Method:** Defines intervals (axis-aligned hyper-rectangles) as certifications: sound certifications ensure robustness within an interval, while complete certifications guarantee prediction change outside the interval. Develops lattice traversal operators and a refine & verify iterative scheme using formal MLP verifiers. Proves polynomial-time solvability for complete certification optimization but strong intractability for sound certifications. Introduces ParallelepipedoNN for empirical evaluation. **Results:** Formal guarantees for maximality (sound) and minimality (complete) certifications. Asymmetries in optimization complexity demonstrated, with logarithmic algorithms for symmetric intervals. **Impact:** Establishes a new theoretical foundation for adversarial robustness, opens questions about tractability trade-offs, and provides tools for formal verification in safety-critical ML systems.

**Overview:** Proposes GATS, a planning framework for LLM agents that eliminates LLM calls during inference by combining UCB1-based tree search with a layered world model. Addresses inefficiencies and stochasticity in prior methods like LATS and ReAct. **Method:** Three-layer world model: (L1) exact symbolic action matching, (L2) execution log statistics, and (L3) LLM-based prediction for unknown actions. Uses systematic tree search with learned priors to guide exploration. **Results:** Achieves 100% success rate on synthetic tasks and 12 challenging stress tests, outperforming LATS (88.9%) and ReAct (23.9%). Requires zero LLM calls per task during planning (vs. 37 for LATS) and produces deterministic plans with zero variance. **Impact:** Demonstrates that systematic search with learned models can surpass LLM-guided exploration, advancing agent planning efficiency and reliability. Highlights the potential of decoupling planning from LLM inference for deterministic, high-performance agents.

**Overview:** This paper proposes a privacy-preserving architecture for intent-based network management in 6G, addressing conflicts between regulatory constraints (e.g., GDPR) and traditional intent verification methods requiring deep packet inspection. **Method:** The architecture provisions resources based on intent categories without traffic inspection and verifies fulfilment using only aggregate PM counters at the O1 interface. **Overview:** Addresses the critical bottleneck of token-generation latency in reasoning LLMs and agentic systems, where per-token latency directly impacts end-to-end response time. **Method:** Proposes Synchronization-Free All-Reduce (SiFAR), which eliminates synchronization overheads in Tensor Parallelism (TP) by (1) dual buffering to remove WAW dependencies in oneshot All-Reduce, (2) redundant pull via in-switch reduction for scalable TP, and (3) speculative reduction to pre-initiate data transfer before top barriers. **Results:** SiFAR reduces All-Reduce latency by up to 52% and improves end-to-end throughput by 18.6% for Llama-3.1-8B and 13.1% for Qwen3.5-397B-17B at TP=8. **Impact:** Advances low-latency LLM inference by addressing synchronization bottlenecks in distributed TP, enabling faster agentic systems and reasoning models.

### [Signed Symmetric Quantization: Mitigating Clipping in Few-Bit Integer Representations](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08779)

<small>**cs.LG updates on arXiv.org** &nbsp;·&nbsp; 2026-07-13 05:00:00 &nbsp;·&nbsp; `quantization` `integer-arithmetic` `large-language-models` `memory-efficiency`</small>

**Overview:** This paper identifies a critical source of quantization error in few-bit signed integer representations: clipping of positive outliers due to the standard symmetric quantizer's fixed positive scale. It proposes signed symmetric quantization as a runtime-efficient alternative to asymmetric quantization. **Method:** The authors introduce the signed absmax grid, which places the extra representable negative value on the dominant-outlier tail via a sign selection rule while keeping the zero point at zero. They prove conditional bound-optimality on ℓ₂ quantization error and show the condition holds for 88-99% of weight groups in pre-trained LLMs at low bit widths. **Results:** Empirical validation on Qwen3, Qwen3.5, and Llama3 models shows improved perplexity and few-shot accuracy over unsigned symmetric quantization at no extra inference cost. **Impact:** Advances memory-efficient quantization for LLMs by addressing a fundamental limitation in signed integer representations, with implications for deployment on edge devices.
